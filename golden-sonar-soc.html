What is a Security Operation Center (SOC)?

A Security Operation Center (SOC) is a centralized team — often supported by technology, processes, and tools — that monitors, detects, investigates, and responds to cybersecurity threats and incidents in real time. Think of it like a command center where security experts watch over your IT systems 24/7 to make sure they are safe.

Purpose:
  1. Monitoring – Constantly watch all your systems, networks, servers, endpoints, etc. for signs of suspicious activity.
  2. Threat Detection – Identify and detect real cyber threats (e.g., hacking attempts, malware infections, insider threats).
  3. Incident Response – When something suspicious happens, the SOC investigates it, confirms if it’s real, and acts quickly to stop it.
  4. Threat Intelligence – Use knowledge from global and local sources to stay ahead of new types of cyberattacks.
  5. Reporting and Compliance – Generate reports about security events for management and for meeting regulatory requirements (like ISO, GDPR, etc.).
How a SOC Works:
  • Collect data from many sources (logs, firewalls, antivirus, cloud services, etc.).
  • Analyze the data using security tools like SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) systems.
  • Alert when something suspicious is detected.
  • Investigate to understand the threat.
  • Respond to contain and eliminate the threat.
  • Review and continuously improve security measures.
Why Organizations Need a SOC:
  • Cyberattacks are growing and becoming more sophisticated.
  • 24/7 protection is critical — threats can happen anytime.
  • Early detection and fast response minimize damage and costs.
  • Meet compliance and legal requirements.
  • Build trust with customers and partners.
SOC Setup Types:
No Type Description
1 Internal SOC Company builds and runs its own SOC. Full control but expensive.
2 Managed SOC (SOC-as-a-Service) Outsource SOC services to a third-party provider. Faster, cheaper, scalable.
3 Hybrid SOC Combine internal resources with external experts.
